Ram Dass

From Harvard Professor to Yoga Teacher

"Be here now."-Ram Dass


A Timeline of Ram Dass' Spiritual Journey

1931

Richard Alpert is born in Boston, MA into a wealthy Jewish family

1958

After graduating from Stanford with a doctorate in Psychology, Richard accepted a professor position at Harvard and went on to become the youngest tenured professor at Harvard at that time

1960

Richard meets Timothy Leary, a fellow professor at Harvard, through his work on the exploration of consciousness. Richard and Tim begin studying consciousness together with the help of (psilocybin) mushrooms.

1963

Richard and Timothy Leary are kicked out of Harvard; Dass for administering psilocybin to an undergraduate student and Leary for not giving required lectures.

1964

Richard and Tim Leary move in together, continue to study psychedelics together and eventually publish "The Psychedlic Expericence" with their findings.

1967

Disgraced, depressed, and spritually unsatisfied with what his life had become, Richard traveled to India, on a whim, and eventually meets Bhagavan Das (aka Kermit Michael Riggs) who introduced him to the man that would ultimately become his guru, Neem Karoli Baba, a Hindu Saint, at Kainchi ashram. At this ashram, Richards experiences many things that he can't explain.

1970

Richard stays at the ashram and studies Hinduism, Buddhism, meditation, and Yoga. He begins to witness a transformation in himself and his consciousness. He finds that the practices he has developed in India reproduced the positive affects that psychedelics had on him. Because of his transformation, Richard's guru gifted him with a new name - "Ram Dass"

1971

Ram Dass begins writing a book about what he's learned in India to share with the Western world

1973

The book that Ram Dass has been writing is published. It is titled "Be Here Now", a lesson that he originally learned from Bhagavan Das. The book goes on to sell some 200,000 copies and is coveted as a spiritual manual for many Americans on spiritual journeys during the 1970s

1974 - 1997

Now famous due to his book being a wild success, Ram Dass travels the country giving lectures on Yoga and spirituality and continues to write more books on the same subjects. He does this until he unexpectedly has a stroke in 1997

1997 - 2020

The stroke left Dass with very limited mobility and communication abilities. He admits that at first this was frustrating for him but he now interprets the stroke as an act of grace and an opportunity for him to get closer to God. He continued to offer teachings to the millions of souls he has reached until he passed on in 2020
